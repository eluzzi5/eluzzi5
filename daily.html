Digging for Campus Culture

Project for Tufts Enigma Independent Data Journal - 2018

Skills Used

  • Data Analysis

Tools Used

  • Tableau
  • Voyant
  • Word trees

This was a project for Enigma, an independent, student-run data journal at Tufts University. As inclusivity and the idea of gender as a non-binary concept have become more visible in recent years, especially on college campuses, I wanted to examine whether or not these cultural shifts were represented in language. Media is both a representation of the current state of language and a tool for changing language.


Using the archives of the Tufts Daily independent newspaper, I examined the use of gender pronouns over multiple decades. I explored the he/him/his, she/her/hers, and they/them/theirs pronoun series and their contexts within articles, as well as keywords relating to gender identity.


I used Tableau and Voyant to examine the frequency counts of certain keywords over multiple years.


In 1999, keywords relating to gender were scarce.

By the late 2000s, there is a breadth of gender-related keywords.



1999 2017

I used the Jason Davies Word Tree tool to analyze certain keywords in context. This tool provides a visualization of all instances of a keyword and its contexts.



Finally, I used Tableu’s Sentiment Analysis tool to analyze the sentiment scores for keywords in different article contexts.


First, I looked at differences in sentiment between the phrases "gender-affirmation" and "gender-reassignment," as there has been an increased push to use the former term.




Difference in sentiment of articles using "gender affirmation" versus "gender reassignment."


Next, I looked at the sentiment of keywords relating to gender neutral bathrooms, as this topic has proven controversial.





Below is the sentiment of the word "bathroom" in the context of multiple articles. This points to its controversial nature.



Looking more closely at points on the graph, we can see which articles evoked which sentiments.
